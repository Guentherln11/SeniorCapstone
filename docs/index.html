THE BERRY SITE

Located in Morganton, Burke County, the Berry Site is identified as the Native American town of Joara, occupied from around AD 1400 through 1600. The people of the western Piedmont region of North Carolina lived here for 12,000 years. The broad floodplain of the area on which the Berry Site sits was also lived on for thousands of years, with evidence of pottery and projectile points.

It is also the site for a 16th century Spanish colonial outpost. It was established in 1567 by Captain Juan Pardo and his army, making it the first European settlement in the interior of what is now the United States. The Spanish town was called Cuenca, and a fort was built known as Fort San Juan. This outpost was part of an effort to establish a permanent route of colonial outposts along However, as the result of rising tensions between the Natives of Joara and the Spanish explorers, the settlement only lasted until June of 1568, when it was burned and destroyed.
